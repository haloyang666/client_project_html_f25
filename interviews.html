Interview Guide

The job interview is one of the most crucial steps in your career search process. It is the organization’s opportunity to determine mutual fit. Preparation is key – below are strategies and resources to help you succeed.

The Big Three Questions

  • Why You? – Articulate a strong response to "Tell me about yourself."
  • Why This Position? – Understand the role through job descriptions and research.
  • Why This Organization? – Demonstrate knowledge of the company’s values, goals, and challenges.

Tell Me About Yourself (TMAY)

TMAY is one of the most common interview questions. Think of it as an extended elevator pitch that highlights your identity, experiences, and passion for the role. Keep it concise (under 2 minutes), structured, and tailored to the interviewer’s needs.

  • Introduce yourself (name, program, or area of expertise).
  • Briefly discuss past and current relevant work.
  • End with future goals and how they align with the role.

Preparing for Technical Interviews

Technical interviews assess your problem-solving, coding, and analytical skills. Use the STAR method (Situation, Task, Action, Result) to answer technical and behavioral questions.

Practice platforms include: LeetCode, HackerRank, Big Interview, and Interviewing.io.
